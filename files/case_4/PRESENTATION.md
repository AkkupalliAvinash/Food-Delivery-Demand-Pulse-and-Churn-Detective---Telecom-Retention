# Case 4: Churn Detective

## 5-Slide Presentation Outline

---

## SLIDE 1: The Problem

**Title:** We're Losing Customers Faster Than We Think

**Content:**

- Current churn: 40% (customers leaving)
- Industry benchmark: 1.5% per month
- We're churning 2-3x faster than normal
- Key question: Who leaves, why, and can we keep them?

**Key stat to show:**

- 40% of customers churn = huge problem
- Cost: Each customer = acquisition cost lost

**Visual:** Red alarm icon + "40% CHURN" in large text

---

## SLIDE 2: Who Churns Most?

**Title:** 3 Factors That Predict Churn

**Content:**

**Factor 1: Contract Type** (Biggest impact!)

- Month-to-month: 45% churn
- 1-year: 10% churn
- 2-year: 5% churn
  → Lock-in works! 2-year contracts churn 9x LESS

**Factor 2: Tenure (How long they've been with us)**

- Months 0-6: 45% churn (CRITICAL!)
- Months 6-12: 25% churn
- Over 12 months: 5% churn
  → First 6 months decide everything

**Factor 3: Support Calls**

- 1-2 calls/3mo: 10% churn
- 3-5 calls/3mo: 25% churn
- 5+ calls/3mo: 45% churn
  → More support calls = more distressed customers

**Visual:**

- 3 vertical bar charts showing each factor
- Color code: Red = high churn, Green = low churn

---

## SLIDE 3: Customer Segments (Not All Churners Are The Same)

**Title:** We Have 3 Different Types of Churners - Each Needs Different Help

**Content:**

| Segment              | Size  | Problem                                        | Signal                       |
| -------------------- | ----- | ---------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------- |
| **Fiber-Frustrated** | 1,200 | Fiber internet + too many support calls        | $90/mo, 5 calls/month        |
| **At-Risk New**      | 1,000 | New customer, month-to-month, still evaluating | <6 months old, no commitment |
| **Stable Loyal**     | 4,800 | Generally happy (good news!)                   | 20+ months, 2-year contract  |

**Key insight:**

- Can't use one retention offer for everyone
- Fiber people need support; new people need lock-in; loyal need rewards

**Visual:**

- 3 boxes or circles, each labeled with segment name
- Color coding, customer counts, main problem for each

---

## SLIDE 4: Where Retention Money Should Go

**Title:** 3 Retention Plays - Ranked by ROI

**Content:**

**🥇 PLAY 1: Fiber Support Escalation** (Best ROI)

- **Target:** 1,200 fiber customers with 3+ support calls
- **Offer:** Dedicated support rep + 1 free month
- **Cost:** ~$400/customer/year
- **Expected save rate:** 70% → 980 saved customers
- **Revenue protected:** ~$267,000 (18 months)
- **Why it works:** They're struggling; help today = loyalty tomorrow

**🥈 PLAY 2: New Customer Lock-in**

- **Target:** 1,000 new customers (months 0-6) on month-to-month
- **Offer:** Switch to annual contract = 20% discount
- **Cost:** ~$240/customer/year
- **Expected save rate:** 50% → 500 saved customers
- **Revenue protected:** ~$142,000 (18 months)
- **Why it works:** Lock them in before they leave

**🥉 PLAY 3: Budget Bundle**

- **Target:** 1,000 low-bill customers ($50/month) with churn signals
- **Offer:** Free streaming add-on or $10/month discount (3 months)
- **Cost:** ~$30/customer total
- **Expected save rate:** 40% → 400 saved customers
- **Revenue protected:** ~$73,000 (18 months)
- **Why it works:** They feel they're getting value; stay longer

**Total Impact:** ~$482,000 protected revenue over 18 months

**Visual:**

- 3 boxes with offer details
- Simple visual: Cost vs Revenue Protected comparison
- Total protected: $482K highlighted

---

## SLIDE 5: Implementation Plan & Next Steps

**Title:** How to Make This Real - 30-Day Pilot

**Content:**

**Week 1: Test and Measure**

- Select 20% of each segment
- Run Play 1, Play 2, Play 3 simultaneously
- Measure: Baseline 30-day retention

**Week 2-4: Monitor & Adjust**

- Track actual save rates vs predictions
- If a play underperforms: pause and investigate
- Adjust offers if needed

**Key metrics to track:**

- Save rate by segment (target: 15-35%)
- Revenue protected vs cost spent
- Customer satisfaction scores

**Approval threshold:**

- Need 2x ROI minimum to scale
- If underperforming: investigate + adjust
- If overperforming: double down

**After pilot:**

- Roll out to 100% of target segments
- Retrain model monthly (new churners emerge)
- Monitor for feature drift

**Visual:**

- Simple timeline: Week 1 → Week 2-4 → After pilot
- Decision tree: If X → then Y
