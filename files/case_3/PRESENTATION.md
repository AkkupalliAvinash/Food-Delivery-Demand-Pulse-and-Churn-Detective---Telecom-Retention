# Case 3: Food Delivery Demand Pulse

## 5-Slide Presentation Outline

---

## SLIDE 1: The Problem

**Title:** Where Is Our Surge Policy Breaking?

**Content:**

- Current state: Surge applied to 23.4% of orders
- The problem: 13% of surge happens during LOW-demand times
- Cost: ~₹11,000 per month wasted
- Question: When should surge ACTUALLY fire?

**Visual:** Screenshot of heatmap showing wrong times with surge

---

## SLIDE 2: What We Found - Demand Patterns

**Title:** Demand Peaks at Lunch and Dinner (Not Mid-Afternoon!)

**Content:**

- Lunch peak: 12 PM - 2 PM (1 PM is strongest)
- Dinner peak: 7 PM - 10 PM (8 PM is strongest)
- Mid-afternoon: Looks busy but only 60% of true peak demand
- Weekends 30% busier than weekdays (especially dinner)

**Visual:**

- Chart 1: Hourly demand heatmap
- Chart 2: Weekend vs weekday comparison

---

## SLIDE 3: The City Insight

**Title:** Most Cities Are Identical - Except One

**Content:**

- Delhi, Mumbai, Bangalore, Pune, Hyderabad, Chennai: All same pattern
- All follow lunch (12-2 PM) + dinner (7-10 PM)
- KOLKATA IS DIFFERENT: Lunch peak nearly as strong as dinner
- Implication: Policy should be city-specific only for Kolkata

**Visual:**

- Line chart: All cities overlaid (showing similarity)
- Line chart: Kolkata separated (showing different pattern)

---

## SLIDE 4: Where Surge Is Wasted

**Title:** We're Incentivizing Riders When They Aren't Needed

**Content:**

- Surge happening in low-demand hours:
  - Mid-afternoon (3-5 PM): 20% fewer orders than peaks
  - Late evening (10 PM+): Demand already dropping
- Result: Wasted rider incentives, no customer benefit
- Simple fix: Turn surge off during off-peak hours

**Visual:**

- Scatter plot: Surge placement vs actual demand
- Highlight wasted surge regions

---

## SLIDE 5: Recommendations & Impact

**Title:** 3 Simple Plays - Combined Savings ₹11,000/Month

**Content:**

**Play 1: Remove Mid-Afternoon Surge** ✓

- Turn off surge 3 PM - 5 PM (no real demand)
- Savings: ₹5,000/month

**Play 2: Strengthen True Peaks** ✓

- Increase surge during actual lunch & dinner windows
- Better coverage when customers need it most
- Savings: ₹4,000/month (by reducing wasteful boost elsewhere)

**Play 3: Kolkata Strategy** ✓

- Run lunch boost in Kolkata ONLY
- Other cities don't need lunch surge
- Savings: ₹2,000/month (focused spending)

**Next Steps:**

- Test in 1-2 cities for 1 week
- Measure: delivery times, customer satisfaction
- If good → roll out to all cities

**Visual:**

- 3 boxes showing each play
- Impact chart: ₹11k total monthly savings
